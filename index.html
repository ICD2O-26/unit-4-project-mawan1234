Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) & Wearables

My name is Ayaan and today I will explain to you about the Internet of Medical Things (IoMT), and I will be talking about relevant wearables.

What is the technology?

The internet for medical things refers to a specialized network, where medical devices are connected to the internet and can communicate with each other. These devices collect health data and send it to doctors or medical and health systems to be analyzed, diagnosed, and treated through the internet. An important distinction from IoMT to the normal health app on your phone is that the results on it are only visible to you, if it was part of IoMT the data would be collected and sent to medical professionals or health systems.

Wearables

Wearables are how IoMT is applied in the real world. They come in different forms, however typically they come in the form of smartwatches. But they also come in the form of glucose monitors, inhalers, heart trackers, and more. The usual parts of a wearable compose of: 1. A sensor measures things like heart rates, movement, temperature, and blood oxygen, it's the part of the device that actually collects data. 2. The processor, the processor is the part that converts all the raw data from the sensors into actual information. 3. The connectivity module, this module sends the data from your wearable to your phone. 4. The battery, this is just where the device gets its energy from. 5. The software, the software is like the operating system of the wearable. 6. The user interface is where the device communicates its info to you, ex: the screen on a smart watch. 7. The cloud, the cloud is where all the info and data is sent and that is where it is seen by medical professionals and health systems. Just to note, wearables aren't the only things that use IoMT, even hospital equipment has started using IoMT, like X-rays and pumps.

Benefits of IoMT

IoMT has a lot of benefits, such as the fact that doctors can track and monitor your health in real-time. This helps health problems to be discovered early. IoMT is also convenient and efficient because you don't have to go to the hospital as often as you would otherwise

Challenges and Risks

IoMT also has some problems though. Some wearables are expensive so not everyone has access to IoMT. Naturally you also need to charge the wearable often or you'll have no charging. And not all wearables are exact, they mostly give estimates in their info and they can be innacurate. A big problem though with IoMT is data security, if the systems aren't secure your personal health info can be leaked or hacked. Since the wearables all use the internet, hackers can potentially access your health data. There are countermeasures to this happening, like all health data becoming scrambled and encrypted while being sent, and only authorized devices can read the code, theirs also firewalls and secure authentication alongside a lot of other counter measures

Future

The future of IoMT is largely hinged on AI, AI will be more incorporated in IoMT. AI will in real-time analyze the data coming from the wearable, it will help doctors make decisions, and it will be used to detect patterns in your health that doctors just can't. Wearables are also planned to become smaller, more comfortable, and more accurate.
